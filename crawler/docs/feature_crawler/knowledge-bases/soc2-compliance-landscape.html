Compliance Software by 2030

The SOC 2 compliance automation market has matured rapidly since 2020, growing from a handful of startups into a well-defined category with clear leaders, challengers, and emerging disruptors. The core SOC 2 automation segment was valued at approximately $850M in 2025 and is projected to reach $2.7B by 2028, representing roughly a 3x expansion in three years.

The broader compliance automation tools market was valued at $4.8B in 2024 and is projected to reach $12.3B by 2033 at a 13.2% CAGR. The even broader compliance software market sits at $36.2B in 2025, heading toward $65.8B by 2030 at a 12.7% CAGR. AI-based compliance automation specifically is valued at $6.1B and is expected to reach $18.3B by 2033 at a 14.6% CAGR.

Key demand drivers include: expanding regulatory requirements, customer/enterprise procurement demands for SOC 2 reports, increasing cloud adoption, the shift-left of security into product development, and the growing acceptance of AI-driven evidence collection and continuous monitoring as viable replacements for manual audit preparation.

Traditional Audit Firms

SOC 2 reports must be issued by a licensed CPA firm. The audit itself cannot be fully automated -- a human auditor must attest. This creates a durable, complementary market between automation platforms (which prepare you for the audit) and CPA firms (which conduct and issue the report). Some platforms like Thoropass bundle the audit; most do not.

Big 4 Accounting Firms

The Big 4 collectively generated $219B in revenue in 2025. While they dominate public company audits (Deloitte, EY, PwC, KPMG audit the vast majority of Fortune 500 and S&P 500 companies), their role in SOC 2 specifically is concentrated in large enterprise engagements. Most startups and mid-market companies use specialized or regional firms for SOC 2 due to cost.

Emerging: AI-Native & Open Source Approaches

AI-Native Compliance Platforms

A new wave of AI-native compliance tools is emerging that goes beyond the "automation" of incumbent platforms (which primarily automate evidence collection and monitoring) to use LLMs and AI agents for policy generation, risk assessment, control mapping, and even audit preparation. The AI-based compliance automation segment is valued at $6.1B (2025) and growing at 14.6% CAGR toward $18.3B by 2033.

Breakout Newcomer

Comp AI (Open Source)

  • Founded: Early 2025 by Lewis Carhart, Claudio Fuentes, Mariano Fuentes
  • Funding: $2.6M pre-seed (OSS Capital + Grand Ventures)
  • Growth: $1M ARR in 4 months with 6-person team; 4,000+ companies by early 2026
  • Model: Open-source core with paid services. 500+ integrations.
  • AI capabilities: AI agents automate evidence collection, risk assessments, vendor onboarding. AI Agent Studio for custom automation. Claims 90% of compliance process automatable.
  • Frameworks: SOC 2, ISO 27001, HIPAA, GDPR, 25+ total
  • Why it matters: First credible open-source competitor to Vanta/Drata. Fastest-growing entrant in the space. Community-contributed control templates and framework mappings.
  • Angel investors: David Cramer (Sentry founder), founders from Freshworks, Deel, Pipe
AI-First

Delve

  • AI agents that "eliminate compliance busywork"
  • Focused on automated compliance for AI companies and startups
  • Supports SOC 2, HIPAA, GDPR, ISO 27001
  • Positioning: compliance for the AI era
Incumbent AI Features

AI Capabilities of Existing Leaders

  • Vanta: AI Agent features for automated remediation and risk assessment. Plus plan includes expanded AI Agent capabilities.
  • Drata: Self-describes as "AI-native GRC platform." Autopilot features for automated control testing and remediation.
  • Secureframe: "Comply AI" (launched 2025) drafts policies, generates risk assessments, suggests control implementations based on tech stack.
  • Scytale: "Scy" AI GRC agent. G2 Best Software Award winner in GRC (2026).
  • Sprinto: AI-powered continuous monitoring and automated evidence mapping.
Market AI Trends

Where AI is Transforming SOC 2

  • Policy generation: LLMs auto-draft security policies tailored to org structure and tech stack
  • Evidence collection: AI agents continuously gather and map evidence to controls
  • Risk assessment: Automated risk scoring and prioritization
  • Gap analysis: AI identifies missing controls and recommends remediations
  • Vendor questionnaire automation: AI fills out security questionnaires using existing compliance data
  • Continuous monitoring: ML-based anomaly detection for compliance drift
  • Auditor preparation: AI generates audit-ready documentation packages
  • Estimated time savings: AI cuts compliance work by ~50% (industry claims)

Open Source & DIY Alternatives

Open Source

Comp AI

The leading open-source compliance platform (detailed above). Open-source core on GitHub. Community-contributed control templates, framework mappings, and automations. Paid tiers for managed services. 500+ integrations. The "Red Hat of compliance."

Open Source

Probo

Open-source compliance platform built specifically for startups pursuing SOC 2. Available on GitHub (getprobo/probo). Hybrid model: open-source software paired with hands-on service to eliminate DIY burden while preserving open-source benefits. Early stage but represents the growing open-source compliance movement.

Open Source

Comply (by StrongDM)

SOC 2-focused compliance automation framework on GitHub (strongdm/comply). Features: markdown-powered document pipeline for auditor-friendly policy documents, ticketing integration, open-source policy and procedure templates. Good for engineering-first teams that want to own their compliance tooling. Lighter-weight than full platforms.

DIY Approach

Build Your Own

  • Cost: $0 software + $10K-$25K audit fees + significant internal time
  • Tools: Spreadsheets, Jira/Linear for tracking, Google Docs for policies, manual evidence screenshots
  • Viable for: Very small teams (<20 people) with strong security engineering
  • Drawback: 200-400 hours of manual work vs. 40-80 hours with automation platform
  • Risk: Policy gaps, missed controls, longer audit cycles, no continuous monitoring
  • When it makes sense: Budget-constrained seed-stage startup needing Type I only, with a security-savvy CTO

M&A, Consolidation & Market Dynamics

Key Transactions

$3B Acquisition

Hg Capital acquires AuditBoard (2024)

Private equity firm Hg acquired AuditBoard for $3 billion, the largest transaction in the compliance automation space. AuditBoard was subsequently rebranded to Optro (March 2026) with modular offerings for SOX, compliance, risk, and audit. Validates the massive enterprise GRC market opportunity. AuditBoard continues to operate independently under Hg ownership.

Strategic Acquisition

OneTrust acquires Tugboat Logic (Sep 2021)

OneTrust acquired Tugboat Logic to add SOC 2 and security certification automation to its privacy and GRC platform. Tugboat Logic's proprietary evidence framework (50+ frameworks) was integrated into OneTrust Certification Automation. Tugboat Logic effectively ceased to exist as a standalone product, becoming a module within OneTrust's broader platform.

Buyout/LBO

Expent acquires Hyperproof (Oct 2025)

Hyperproof was acquired in a buyout/LBO by Expent in October 2025. Details of the transaction were not disclosed. This came after Hyperproof's revenue grew from $8.2M (2023) to $21.2M (2024), suggesting a growth-stage acquisition at a revenue multiple.

Tuck-in Acquisition

Scytale acquires AudITech ($15M, 2025)

Scytale acquired AudITech for $15M to expand its compliance automation capabilities. A sign that even smaller players in this space are consolidating to build more complete offerings.

Market Dynamics & Trends

Consolidation Thesis

Platform vs. Point Solution

  • The market is shifting from "SOC 2 tool" to "GRC platform"
  • Leaders are adding frameworks aggressively (Vanta: 30+, Drata: 20+)
  • Multi-framework customers have higher ACV and lower churn
  • Expect more acquisitions as PE firms see the recurring revenue opportunity
  • The AuditBoard/Hg deal ($3B) set the ceiling for GRC automation valuations
Vertical Expansion

Beyond SOC 2

  • Trust Centers: Vanta, Drata building customer-facing trust portals
  • Vendor Risk Management: Expanding from internal compliance to supply chain risk
  • AI Governance: ISO 42001, EU AI Act compliance (Scytale leading here)
  • Questionnaire Automation: Automating security questionnaire responses for sales enablement
  • Risk Management: Full enterprise risk registers and quantification
  • Continuous Compliance: Moving from point-in-time audits to always-on monitoring
Pricing Pressure

Race to the Bottom?

  • Entry-level pricing compressed from ~$15K (2021) to ~$7.5K (2026)
  • Sprinto and open-source alternatives pushing prices lower
  • Comp AI (open source) is the most disruptive pricing threat
  • Leaders respond by moving upmarket and increasing ACV via bundling
  • Vanta's ACV increased from $14K to $18K by selling multi-framework bundles
  • Audit fees remain a separate, largely uncompressed cost (except Thoropass)
SOC 2 Standard Evolution

2025-2026 Updates

  • Reports with 150+ security controls grew from 16% to 23%
  • Confidentiality criteria included in 64.4% of reports (up from 34% in 2023)
  • Availability criteria now in 75.3% of reports
  • Organizations expanding scope to address broader client requirements
  • SOC 2 increasingly a table-stakes requirement, not a differentiator

H2: Open-source (Comp AI) will capture 10-15% of new SOC 2 adopters by 2028, pressuring margins of all incumbents.
H3: At least one more Big 4 or large PE firm will acquire a compliance automation platform by end of 2027.
H4: AI will commoditize the "evidence collection + monitoring" layer, shifting value to audit network effects, trust centers, and vendor risk intelligence.
H5: The Thoropass "audit-included" model will be copied by 2-3 other platforms within 18 months as they acquire or partner with CPA firms.
H6: SOC 2 will become a table-stakes checkbox (not a differentiator) for B2B SaaS by 2028, shifting the competitive landscape to speed-to-compliance and ongoing monitoring quality.
