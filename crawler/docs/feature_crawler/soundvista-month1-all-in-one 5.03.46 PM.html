Product Requirements Document

Make Sound Vista feel believable in one walkthrough.

The product goal for Month 1 is not breadth. It is confidence. A stakeholder should move through the app, feel the atmosphere, understand the room structure, open content, and leave convinced the foundation is real.

immersive first clear navigation reliable playback screenshot-ready

Problem

The repo contains promising visual and 3D pieces, but the active application shell does not yet prove a coherent product experience.

Goal

Turn the existing code into a stakeholder-facing front-end milestone that can support Month 2 data and creator features without redesigning the shell.

A user lands in a branded 3D Sound Vista lobby, understands what to click, enters at least two distinct rooms, opens a piece of content, uses the player controls confidently, and sees a layout that still feels considered on smaller screens.
1

Arrive

First impression is atmospheric and specific to Sound Vista.

2

Navigate

Room selection is obvious and each destination has a reason to exist.

3

Open content

Cards feel like real content modules instead of anonymous placeholders.

4

Control playback

The player feels stable enough that a walkthrough does not turn into debugging.

Experience Principles

How the month should feel

Identity over genericity

Use the Figma palette and typographic tone. Avoid default dashboard aesthetics.

Spatial clarity

The user can always tell where they are, what they can do next, and how to go back.

Confidence through stability

No speculative feature work if it weakens the reliability of the month-1 demo path.

Priority Stack

What matters first

P0

3D lobby integration, route differentiation, mock-data extraction, playback state, responsive layouts.

P1

Extra polish that directly improves the walkthrough: transitions, stronger room identity, cleaner shell details.

P2

Anything from later months: auth, real data, uploads, search, notifications, analytics, web3, social systems.

Scope risk

  • Trying to sneak in auth or real data work.
  • Designing brand-new systems instead of integrating what already exists.
  • Treating room count as URL count instead of experience count.

Implementation risk

  • Leaving player logic trapped in legacy components.
  • Shipping one layout that only works on desktop.
  • Polishing before route/data/player fundamentals are solid.
