## Research Personas, Synthetic Respondents, and Simulated Populations

### 16. Park et al. - Generative Agents: Interactive Simulacra of Human Behavior

- Type: Research / agent architecture paper
- URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2304.03442
- Used for: strongest positive evidence that persona-like agents can appear believable in bounded worlds

### 17. Aher et al. - Using Large Language Models to Simulate Multiple Humans and Replicate Human Subject Studies

- Type: Research / synthetic respondents
- URL: https://proceedings.mlr.press/v202/aher23a.html
- Used for: evidence that LLM agents can reproduce some human-subject-study results while still introducing realism distortions

### 18. Can LLMs Replace Economic Choice Prediction Labs? The Case of Language-based Persuasion Games

- Type: Research / economic choice simulation
- URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.17435
- Used for: direct negative evidence that LLMs fail as substitutes in more strategic, language-rich economic settings

### 19. ChatGPT is not A Man, but Das Man

- Type: Research / representation critique
- URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.02919
- Used for: argument that LLM-generated populations flatten diversity and underrepresent minority viewpoints

### 20. An Analysis of Large Language Models for Simulating User Responses in Surveys

- Type: Research / survey simulation
- URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.06874
- Used for: evidence that survey simulation quality is limited by viewpoint bias and may require diversification methods

### 21. LLM Generated Persona is a Promise with a Catch

- Type: Research / cross-domain synthesis
- URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.16527
- Used for: broad framing of the field as promising but structurally limited
