# Papangelis (2025) — The Synthetic Persona Fallacy

**URL:** https://interactions.acm.org/blog/view/the-synthetic-persona-fallacy
**Venue:** ACM Interactions

## Core Concepts

### Synthetic Persona Fallacy
The false assumption that LLM-generated user profiles can substitute for real human research participants. Conflates statistical plausibility with human complexity.

### Bias Laundering
LLMs project statistical averages filtered through cultural/economic/geographic bias, then wrap them in empathy language — making the bias harder to detect and more dangerous.

### Legitimacy Loop
Academic work → commercial marketing → demand for more synthetic research → further legitimation. A self-reinforcing cycle.

### Capability Erosion
Organizations downsizing UX teams in favor of prompt engineering, losing capacity for genuine human inquiry.

## Evidence
- Systematic review of 52 research articles on synthetic persona evaluation
- Documented gap between commercial claims and demonstrated validity
- Training data reflects English-speaking, affluent, tech-literate populations

## Five Failure Modes (via NN/g)
1. Sycophancy/idealization
2. Oversimplification of behavior
3. Shallow undifferentiated needs
4. Inability to generate behavioral data
5. Training data bias

## Key Takeaway
The strongest conceptual framework for understanding WHY synthetic personas fail. "Bias laundering" is the most important concept for our research — it describes how AI personas make bad data look good.
