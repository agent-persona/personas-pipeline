# UXtweak + Slovak University (2025) — Meta-Review of 182 Studies on Synthetic Participants

**URL:** https://arxiv.org/html/2505.09478
**Referenced via:** The Voice of User article

## Overview
The largest systematic review of synthetic participants ever conducted.

## Methodology
- Databases: Scopus, Web of Science, IEEE Xplore, ACM Digital Library
- 4,400 initial publications → 182 meeting quality criteria
- 9 domains: psychology, HCI, social science, education, healthcare, economics, marketing, mobility
- Models: GPT-2 through GPT-4o, LLaMA, Claude, Gemini, Mistral, Qwen, DeepSeek

## Key Findings
- Synthetic participants "fail while looking like they succeed" — clean structure masks absence of causal reasoning
- Card sorting best NMI (Claude): 0.73 (SD 0.11)
- Personality: "unrealistically high and more factorially pure" traits
- Emotional: near-perfect awareness scores (unrealistic); avoidance of jealousy
- LLM test-retest: responses resemble same person; two human participants already better sample
- Prompting improvements: "modest" across all techniques; fundamental problems unchanged
- Stereotyping: minorities cast into flat archetypal roles

## Success Conditions (Limited)
- Hypothesis generation before real fieldwork
- Cognitive pretesting of instruments
- Data augmentation (cautiously)
- Card sorting rough starting points
- Well-documented topics with high training data coverage

## Key Takeaway for Our Experiment
This meta-review provides the definitive landscape. Key design principle: synthetic outputs are hypothesis generators, not evidence. Our experiment should measure the gap between synthetic confidence and actual accuracy.
