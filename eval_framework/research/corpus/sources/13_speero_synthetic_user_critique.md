# Speero — Why I'm Not Sold on Synthetic User Research

**URL:** https://speero.com/post/why-im-not-sold-on-synthetic-user-research
**Author:** Emma Travis (CRO practitioner)

## Core Argument
Synthetic users trade confidence for convenience. AI predicts what is most likely; humans do what is least expected.

## Case Study 1: Diabetes Management Product (Insulin Cooling Device)
- **AI predicted:** price sensitivity, vague marketing, return policy concerns
- **Real users revealed:** (a) already adopted DIY alternatives (ice packs, FRIO sleeves), (b) didn't know insulin storage affects potency → never perceived the product's core value
- **Divergence type:** AI flagged predictable friction; missed WHY users rejected the product entirely

## Case Study 2: Homepage Heatmap
- AI predictive heatmap: heavy interaction with cart, CTA, search, bestsellers, logos
- Microsoft Clarity real data: clicks almost exclusively on site search
- **100% mismatch** in engagement patterns

## Case Study 3: Emotional Depth
Participant revealed years of sleep deprivation, financial struggle, lack of support. Session ended early. Synthetic users cannot simulate this weight.

## What Synthetic Users Miss
- Irrationality and contradiction
- Context-dependent behavior (workarounds, constraints)
- Emotional undertones
- Knowledge gaps (what users don't know they don't know)
- Hesitation and friction moments

## Key Takeaway
The diabetes case study is the perfect illustration for our research: AI identifies surface-level friction while missing the structural reasons for product rejection. Our experiment should include scenarios where the "real" answer requires understanding context, workarounds, or knowledge gaps.
